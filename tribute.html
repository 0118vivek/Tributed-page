A .P .J Abdul kalam

1931-2015

Missile man of india

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, commonly known as APJ Abdul Kalam, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. His father, Jainulabdeen, was an imam of a local mosque and a boat owner, while his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife. He was the youngest of five siblings. Kalam's ancestors were wealthy traders and landowners. Kalam studied physics and aerospace engineering. He began his career as a scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He played a significant role in India’s civilian space program and military missile development. In 2002, Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. He was known as the “People’s President” due to his humility and dedication to public service. His presidency focused on issues such as poverty, agriculture, youth, and rural development. After his presidency, Kalam continued to contribute to society through various initiatives and motivational speaking. He authored several books and received numerous awards and honors throughout his life. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, in Shillong, India. In his memory, several institutions and landmarks have been named after him, including the Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Planetarium and Abdul Kalam Island (formerly Wheeler Island).
